Lowriding

My first car was a 78 monte carlo. I bought it stock in yakima Wa for $4300 drove it back to sf and turned it into a lowrider. After putting in some more money I sold the car for 13K as a lowrider.

My next few cars were these two el caminos, 1979 and 1980. I didnt change any of the suspension, but i did do some engine work and put wire wheels and a bumping sound system in both. I also changed the carpets and headliners. Paid 5K for both and sold them both for 10K.

My next car was a 1970 monte carlo. Found it in arizona and drove it home 12 hours back to San Fransisco. Turned it into a lowrider, and sold it. I paid 18K for the stock car and sold it for 25K as a lowrider.

My current car is a 1961 2 door impala. They call that year the bubbletop because the back glass is like a spaceship. I paid 47K for the car and I have big plans for it. Hydraulics, painted rooftop patterns, and cleaning up the engine compartment are a few of the things i wanna do. This car might be a keeper, but everthing has its price!

Skateboarding

Iv learned that if you can picture it in your mind, you can pretty much do it on a skateboard. Dedicaton, determination, persistence, dealing with pain and failure, blood sweat and tears, overcoming fear and gaining confidence has been engrained in me through skateboading.
